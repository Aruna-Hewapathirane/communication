Categories of Communication

1. Greetings

Words or phrases used to acknowledge the presence of someone or to begin a conversation.

  • Casual greetings: "Hi," "Hey," "Hello."
  • Formal greetings: "Good morning," "Good afternoon," "How do you do?"
  • Time-based greetings: "Good evening," "Good night."
2. Farewells

Words or phrases used when leaving or parting ways with someone.

  • Casual farewells: "Bye," "See you later," "Take care."
  • Formal farewells: "Goodbye," "Farewell," "It was a pleasure meeting you."
3. Orders

Instructions or commands given to direct someone to perform a task.

  • Direct commands: "Do this," "Please send the report."
  • Requests for actions: "Please close the door," "Could you help me?"
4. Requests

Asking for something, typically in a polite or formal manner.

  • Polite requests: "Could you please assist me?" "Would you mind doing this?"
  • Formal requests: "I would like to request your assistance," "I kindly ask for your help."
5. Announcements

Public or formal declarations of information meant to inform others.

  • Event announcements: "The meeting is at 3 PM," "The event has been canceled."
  • Emergency announcements: "Please evacuate the building," "Stay calm, help is on the way."
6. Intentions

Statements expressing plans, desires, or future actions.

  • Plans: "I intend to finish this by tomorrow," "I’m planning to go out later."
  • Desires: "I would like to learn Spanish," "I want to visit Paris one day."
7. Questions and Answers

Communication involving inquiries and responses.

  • Questions: "What time is it?" "Where are you from?"
  • Answers: "It's 3 PM," "I'm from New York."
8. Compliments

Words of praise or admiration.

  • Personal compliments: "You did a great job!" "You look wonderful today!"
  • Professional compliments: "Your presentation was outstanding," "I appreciate your hard work."
9. Apologies

Expressions of regret or acknowledgment of wrongdoing.

  • Casual apologies: "Sorry for the delay," "My bad."
  • Formal apologies: "I sincerely apologize for the inconvenience," "Please accept my apologies."
10. Explanations

Providing clarifications or details to make something clearer.

  • Clarifying: "Let me explain," "This is how it works."
  • Providing details: "The reason I said that is," "Here’s the full story."
11. Suggestions

Offering advice or recommendations for action.

  • Advice: "I suggest you take a break," "Try using this method."
  • Recommendations: "You should check out this book," "Consider this option."
12. Invitations

Inviting others to participate in events or activities.

  • Casual invitations: "Want to grab coffee?" "Join us for lunch!"
  • Formal invitations: "We would be delighted if you could attend the event," "Please accept our invitation to the ceremony."
13. Promises

Committing to something in the future.

  • Simple promises: "I’ll call you later," "I promise I’ll finish it by tomorrow."
  • Formal promises: "I guarantee you that we’ll resolve the issue," "You have my word."
14. Warnings

Communication meant to alert others to potential danger or caution.

  • Dangerous situations: "Be careful, it’s slippery," "There’s a fire exit over here."
  • General warnings: "Don’t forget to lock the door," "Please avoid using your phone while driving."
15. Refusals

Politely turning down a request or invitation.

  • Polite refusals: "Sorry, I can’t help with that," "I must decline the invitation."
  • Formal refusals: "Regrettably, I am unable to comply with your request," "Unfortunately, I have to decline your offer."
